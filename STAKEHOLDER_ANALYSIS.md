# Stakeholder Analysis – Beauty Salon Management Platform

## 1. Purpose
This document identifies key stakeholders involved in the salon management platform, summarizes their objectives, influence, expectations, and potential engagement strategies to ensure successful delivery and adoption.

## 2. Stakeholder Landscape

| Stakeholder Group | Persona Examples | Core Objectives | Influence Level | Interest Level | Engagement Strategy |
|-------------------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Salon Owners / Administrators** | Franchise owners, single-salon entrepreneurs | Drive revenue, maintain brand standards, ensure compliance, manage teams efficiently | High | High | Executive briefings, dashboard demos, quarterly business reviews, tailored analytics |
| **Salon Managers** | Branch managers, operations supervisors | Optimize daily operations, manage staff schedules, monitor KPIs, resolve customer issues | High | High | Weekly ops meetings, targeted training, performance reports, feedback loops |
| **Front Desk / Reception Teams** | Receptionists, customer service specialists | Handle bookings, client communications, and walk-in traffic smoothly | Medium | High | Step-by-step guides, chat support, quick reference materials, real-time alerts |
| **Salon Staff & Technicians** | Stylists, therapists, nail technicians | Deliver services efficiently, manage personal schedules, track customer preferences | Medium | Medium | Mobile-friendly dashboards, in-app notifications, microlearning resources |
| **Customers** | Regular clients, new guests, VIP customers | Seamless booking, transparent pricing, loyalty benefits, prompt communication | Low direct influence | High | Automated updates, intuitive UX, surveys, loyalty program messaging |
| **IT Support & Technical Partners** | Internal IT, managed service providers | Ensure platform reliability, resolve incidents, configure integrations | Medium | Medium | Incident runbooks, system dashboards, release notes, technical workshops |
| **Finance & Accounting Teams** | Tenant accountants, platform finance leads | Accurate payment reconciliation, refund tracking, financial reporting | Medium | High | Access to payment dashboards, scheduled reports, compliance briefings |
| **Super Administrators / Platform Operators** | SaaS operations leads, compliance officers | Onboard tenants, enforce policies, maintain platform health | High | Medium | Governance reviews, strategic roadmap sessions, compliance dashboards |
| **Marketing & Customer Success Teams** | Tenant marketers, platform growth managers | Promote services, track campaigns, improve customer retention | Medium | Medium | Campaign analytics, CRM exports, success stories, targeted outreach |
| **External Partners** | Payment gateways, email/SMS providers, regulators | Deliver supporting services, ensure legal compliance | High (for dependencies) | Medium | SLA management, integration testing, regulatory reporting |

## 3. Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

### 3.1 Salon Owners / Administrators
- **Success Measures:** Profitability, customer retention, staff productivity, regulatory compliance.
- **Pain Points:** Fragmented systems, manual reporting, inconsistent customer experience.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Executive dashboards, KPI alerts, roadmap input sessions, personalized onboarding.

### 3.2 Salon Managers
- **Success Measures:** Operational efficiency, schedule adherence, service quality, customer satisfaction.
- **Pain Points:** Manual scheduling, unexpected staff absences, lack of real-time data.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Operational playbooks, manager-specific training, weekly reports, escalation channels.

### 3.3 Front Desk & Reception Teams
- **Success Measures:** Smooth bookings, reduced wait times, accurate customer communication.
- **Pain Points:** Double bookings, fragmented customer data, manual payment tracking.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Interactive tutorials, quick reference cheat sheets, responsive support, feedback forms.

### 3.4 Salon Staff & Technicians
- **Success Measures:** Clear schedules, minimal downtime, positive customer reviews, accessible client info.
- **Pain Points:** Last-minute changes, limited leave visibility, lack of feedback loops.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Mobile/responsive views, notifications, performance insights, recognition programs.

### 3.5 Customers
- **Success Measures:** Easy booking, transparent pricing, timely reminders, loyalty rewards.
- **Pain Points:** Limited availability, poor communication, inconsistent service quality.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Personalized emails, rewards portal, post-service surveys, referral incentives.

### 3.6 IT Support & Technical Partners
- **Success Measures:** Fast incident resolution, stable integrations, clear observability.
- **Pain Points:** Insufficient logs, unclear ownership, ad-hoc configuration changes.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Detailed runbooks, alerting dashboards, sandbox environments, change management processes.

### 3.7 Finance & Accounting Teams
- **Success Measures:** Accurate ledgers, timely reconciliations, compliant reporting.
- **Pain Points:** Payment mismatches, manual refunds, fragmented financial data.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Scheduled exports, automated alerts for anomalies, compliance workshops.

### 3.8 Super Administrators / Platform Operators
- **Success Measures:** Healthy tenant base, adherence to policies, uptime, scalability.
- **Pain Points:** Onboarding bottlenecks, compliance breaches, lack of visibility.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Governance meetings, system health dashboards, incident retrospectives.

### 3.9 Marketing & Customer Success Teams
- **Success Measures:** Campaign ROI, customer retention, referral growth.
- **Pain Points:** Limited campaign tracking, poor customer segmentation, siloed data.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Marketing dashboards, CRM integrations, co-branded assets, referral analytics.

### 3.10 External Partners
- **Success Measures:** Stable integrations, contractual compliance, mutual growth.
- **Pain Points:** API changes without notice, SLA violations, security concerns.
- **Engagement Tactics:** Integration roadmaps, regular vendor syncs, shared incident reports, audit trails.

## 4. Power-Interest Matrix Summary
- **High Power / High Interest:** Owners/Administrators, Managers, Platform Operators – require collaborative planning and continuous engagement.
- **High Power / Medium Interest:** External partners, IT Support – maintain strong communication and clear SLAs.
- **Medium Power / High Interest:** Reception teams, Finance, Marketing – provide targeted support and transparent updates.
- **Low Power / High Interest:** Customers, Staff – deliver excellent UX, proactive communication, and reward mechanisms.

## 5. Communication Plan Snapshot
- **Monthly Executive Dashboard Reviews** – Owners, Administrators, Platform Operators.
- **Weekly Operational Stand-ups** – Managers, Reception, IT Support.
- **Real-Time Notifications & Chat Support** – Reception, Staff, Customers.
- **Quarterly Compliance & Finance Briefings** – Finance Teams, External Partners.
- **Product Roadmap Webinars** – All tenants, marketing stakeholders, platform operators.

## 6. Risk & Engagement Considerations
- Misalignment between tenant expectations and platform roadmap – mitigate with transparent prioritization and feedback loops.
- Resistance to process change among staff – address with training, incentives, and early wins.
- Dependence on external partners for critical services – manage with strong SLAs and redundancy plans.
- Security and compliance breaches – require proactive monitoring, audits, and rapid communication channels.

## 7. Next Steps
- Validate stakeholder mapping with key representatives.
- Assign relationship owners for each stakeholder group.
- Integrate stakeholder feedback into project roadmap and communication plans.
- Review and update this analysis quarterly or after major product releases.
